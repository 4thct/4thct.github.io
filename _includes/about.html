We are a Midweſtern group of living hiſtorians who ſeek to accurately and reſpectfully interpret the lives of the ſoldiery in the 4th Connecticut Regiment during the American War of Independence for the education of the public by means of ſtrict attention to the material, ſocial, and military cultures of the 18th century.

Brave men willing to free our country from its tyrannical oppreſsion are encouraged to enliſt at once, and ſhall receive a fair wage in Continental dollars, a ſet of clothes, and ſuch arms and accoutrements as are appropriate. Intrepid women of ſimilar diſpoſition may find it profitable to follow the troops offering ſuch ſervices as will help the cauſe (viz. the ſelling of produce and other items, the laundering of clothes, &c).
